# Telecommunications

### Project Overview
This project analyzes a telecom dataset of 7,043 customers to understand why people cancel their subscriptions (customer churn). The main goal is to find the core reasons behind customer losses, identify which products or contract types are losing the most money, and pinpoint exactly when new users are most likely to drop the service. These insights will help the business build better retention plans and protect monthly revenue.

### Key Results
-Total Revenue: The national telecommunication operators generated a total of ₦21bn in revenue across a base of 244K customers.  

-Total Voice Traffic: A total of 12M calls were made across the network.  

-Revenue by Operator: Market revenue is highly balanced among the four major operators:  

°MTN: ₦5.19bn   

°Airtel: ₦5.19bn   

°Glo: ₦5.17bn   

°9Mobile: ₦5.16bn   

-Overall Churn Rate: The subscriber base consists of 195K active users and 49K churned users, reflecting an overall churn rate of approximately 20.05% to 20.5%.  

-Operator Performance: Airtel faces the highest risk with a churn rate of 20.37%.  

-9Mobile maintains the most stability with the lowest churn rate at 19.85%.  

-Despite minor percentage difference, market loss is heavily standardized, with each operator maintaining roughly 49K active subscribers and 12K churned subscribers.  

-The 50+ age group is the primary driver of churn, accounting for 82K inactive users.  

-This group otherwise represents the largest slice of revenue contribution at ₦8.7bn (compared to just ₦2.6bn from subscribers <25).  

-"Super High Value" customers (spending 110–150k) are leaving at the highest volume, with 14.9K users churned. This represents a significant risk to future revenue stability.  

-Gender: Higher churn activity is consistently observed among male subscribers compared to females. Males also drive higher total regional revenue blocks (~₦2.0bn–₦2.1bn per state) compared to females (~₦1.4bn per state).  

-Regions: Lagos and Abuja lead the country in total subscriber volume , while Sokoto and Rivers exhibit the highest overall churn density.  

### Recommendation
-Create a dedicated customer care and retention campaign tailored specifically for subscribers aged 50 and older. This could include simplified service plans, direct phone-support priorities, and targeted loyalty rewards to stop this severe revenue leak.

-Establish an exclusive VIP tier or account-management protocol for these top-tier spenders(Super High value Customer). Offering discounts, bespoke data/voice bundles, this may help protect these high-margin subscribers from migrating to competitors.

-Design marketing and promotional offers that appeal specifically to the usage patterns of the male gender such as heavily optimized business/data packages or sports-bundled subscription to incentivize long-term loyalty.
